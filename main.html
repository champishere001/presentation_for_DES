Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS)

Sample Design, Concepts, and Definitions
Based on Instruction Manual Vol - I (Pages A-3 to A-35)
National Statistics Office, Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation

Sample Design & Rotational Scheme

2

Rotational Panel Scheme

  • A rotational panel sampling design will be used in the survey. [cite: 86]
  • The rotational scheme will be of two years duration to accommodate the changes in the frame in the intercensal period. [cite: 87]
  • Each selected household will be visited four times in four consecutive months—one with first visit schedule and other three with the revisit schedule. [cite: 89]
  • Monthly estimates will be generated for successive months without any break in the series starting from the fourth month, ensuring a 75% matching between two consecutive months. [cite: 90]

Outline of the Design & Frame

3

Sampling Frame

  • The sampling frame for urban sector is the list of Urban Frame Survey (UFS) blocks as per latest Urban Frame Survey. [cite: 145]
  • For rural sector, it is the list of villages as per Census 2011 updated by removing those villages which are urbanized. [cite: 145]

Sub-Unit Formation

  • Sub-units are formed in the UFS blocks with number of households 250 or more. [cite: 151]
  • In villages (with Census 2011 population of 1000 or more), the number of SUs to be formed is decided based on projected present population. [cite: 147]

Stratification of FSUs

4

Basic Stratum Configuration

  • Stratum Type I: Each ‘Category - 1’ district in ‘Group - A’ states/UTs and State Headquarter District of each of the North Eastern states excluding Assam. [cite: 153]
  • Rural Sector: A Special Stratum comprising of all the uninhabited villages as per Census 2011 is formed at All-India level. [cite: 156] From the remaining villages, two more strata are formed: villages within 5 Kms from the district headquarter/city (Stratum 1) and rest of the villages (Stratum 2). [cite: 157, 159]
  • Urban Sector: Each million plus city as per census 2011 will be considered as a separate stratum. [cite: 160] Rest of the urban areas of the district will constitute another stratum. [cite: 161]

Concepts: Population and Household

6

Population Coverage Exclusions

  • Under-trial prisoners in jails and indoor patients of hospitals, nursing homes etc., are to be excluded. [cite: 195]
  • Floating population, i.e., persons without any normal residence will not be listed. [cite: 198]
  • Persons residing in barracks of military and paramilitary forces (like police, BSF, etc.) will be kept outside the survey coverage. [cite: 202]

Definition of Household

  • A group of persons normally living together and taking food from a common kitchen will constitute a household. [cite: 210]
  • It will include temporary stay-aways (total period of absence expected to be less than 6 months) but exclude temporary visitors. [cite: 211]
  • A resident employee, or domestic servant, or a paying guest... will be considered as a member of the household with whom he or she resides. [cite: 219]

Economic Activity

7

Defining Economic Activity

  • Any activity that results in production of goods and services that adds value to national product is considered as an economic activity. [cite: 250]
  • Market activities: involve remuneration to those who perform it, i.e., activity performed for pay or profit. [cite: 252]
  • Non-market activities: activities relating to the primary sector which result in production of primary goods (including free collection), and the activities relating to the own-account production of fixed assets. [cite: 258, 259]
  • The activities like prostitution, begging, etc., which may result in earning, will not be considered as economic activities for the survey. [cite: 262]

Broad Activity Statuses

8
According to this, a person will be in one or a combination of the following three broad activity statuses during a reference period: [cite: 265]
  • Employed (Worker): Working or being engaged in economic activity. [cite: 265]
  • Unemployed: Being not engaged in economic activity (work) and either making tangible efforts to seek 'work' or being available for 'work' if the 'work' is available. [cite: 265]
  • Not in Labour Force: Being not engaged in any economic activity (work) and also not available for 'work'. [cite: 265]
  • Persons who are either 'working' (or employed) or 'seeking or available for work' (or unemployed) during the reference period together constitute the labour force. [cite: 279]

Classification of Workers

9

Self-Employed

  • Persons who operate their own farm or non-farm enterprises... are deemed to be self-employed. [cite: 284]
  • Includes own-account workers (run without hiring labour), employers (run by hiring labour), and helpers in household enterprises. [cite: 288, 290]

Wage & Salaried Employees

  • Regular wage/salaried employee: Persons working in other’s farm or non-farm enterprises... and getting in return salary or wages on a regular basis. [cite: 306]
  • Casual wage labour: A person casually engaged in other’s farm or non-farm enterprises... and getting in return wage according to the terms of the daily or periodic work contract. [cite: 308]

Approaches for Determining Activity Status

10
  • Usual Activity Status: The activity status determined on the basis of the reference period of last 365 days preceding the date of survey. [cite: 312] Uses the major time criterion. [cite: 321]
  • Subsidiary Economic Activity Status: The status in which such economic activity is pursued during the reference period of 365 days preceding the date of survey for 30 days or more. [cite: 327, 328]
  • Current Weekly Status (CWS): The activity status obtaining for a person during a reference period of 7 days preceding the date of survey. [cite: 334] Uses priority cum major time criterion. [cite: 335]
  • Current Daily Status (CDS): The current daily activity status for a person is determined on the basis of his/ her activity status on each day of the reference week using a priority-cum-major time criterion. [cite: 341]

Hours Worked & Earnings

11
  • Hours actually worked: The total time spent by the person on that day towards production of all goods and services that are considered as economic activities. [cite: 359] Includes direct production, related production activity, and short breaks. [cite: 360]
  • Hours would have worked: Corresponds to the hours the person would have worked had he worked in the economic activity from which the person is temporarily off. [cite: 382]
  • Earnings (Regular/Casual): Relates to remuneration in cash and in kind which are paid... relates to employees’ gross remuneration before deductions. [cite: 386, 389]
  • Gross earnings of self-employed: Derived by obtaining total expenses and deducting it from the gross output of that period. [cite: 393]

Training, Land, and Income Classifications

12

Vocational Training

  • Formal Training: Acquired through institutions/ organisations and is recognised by national certifying bodies. [cite: 418]
  • Non-formal Training: Alternative to formal learning and is also structured but is more flexible. [cite: 421]
  • Informal Training: Occurs in daily life, in the family, in the workplace... is not structured. [cite: 424]

Land & Other Incomes

  • Land Possessed: Land owned (including owner like possession) + land leased in - land leased out + land otherwise possessed. [cite: 491]
  • Rent/Interest: Rent received on land... Interest received from deposits, provident fund, dividends. [cite: 500, 514]
  • Remittances: Transfers received by the household (in monetary terms) from a different household located in India or abroad. [cite: 526]
